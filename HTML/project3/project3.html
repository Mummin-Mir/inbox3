,

Sir Muhammad Iqbal (9 November 1877 – 21 April 1938)
was a South Asian Islamic philosopher, poet and politician.
[1][2][3][4] His poetry is considered to be among the greatest
of the 20th century,[5][6][7][8] and his vision of a cultural
and political ideal for the Muslims of British-ruled India[9]
is widely regarded as having animated the impulse for the
Pakistan Movement.[1][10] He is commonly referred to by the
honourific Allama (Persian: علامه, transl. "learned").[11][12]
and widely considered one of the most important and influential
Muslim thinkers and Western religious philosophers of the 20th
century.[13][14][15][16]
